# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Anyone who needs a large on-screen countdown, count-up, or clock overlaid on their
screen: live streamers running it beside OBS, conference/church/stage AV operators
driving a speaker timer, teachers, presenters. Released as a general public tool, so
first-run clarity matters as much as power. The control window lives on a second
monitor or beside the thing being timed; the transparent overlay sits on top of
whatever is being captured or projected.

## Product Purpose

A control panel that configures and drives a transparent, always-on-top overlay
showing a countdown (duration), countdown-to-time, count-up (stopwatch), or live
clock. Users set the time, appearance (font, size, color, outline, position), a
warning threshold that enlarges the text, and an at-zero message. Success = the
operator gets the timer they want on screen in seconds and can start/pause/reset
without hunting.

## Brand Personality

Friendly and approachable, not intimidating. Clear over clever. A tool a first-timer
can open and understand in one glance, that a repeat user can drive by keyboard. Calm
confidence, no pro-audio wall of knobs.

## Anti-references

- Dense pro-AV / DAW panels that greet a newcomer with 40 identical controls and no hierarchy.
- Jargon without explanation ("epoch", "threshold ms").
- Toy/skeuomorphic timer widgets that sacrifice legibility for cuteness.

## Design Principles

- **Legible in one glance.** The primary job (set time -> start) is always the most obvious thing on screen.
- **Teach through the UI.** Labels, hints, and the live preview explain the feature; no manual needed.
- **Predictable controls.** Standard form vocabulary, consistent affordances, keyboard-driven.
- **Friendly, not loud.** Approachable tone without decorative noise; the overlay is the star, the panel disappears into the task.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

General public tool: aim for WCAG AA. Keyboard operability (already has shortcuts),
visible focus states, contrast >=4.5:1 for body/labels, and no meaning by color alone.
